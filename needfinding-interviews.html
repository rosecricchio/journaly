Needfinding and Methodology

Interviews

Another aspect of needfinding we wanted to include was interviews with professionals, such as psychology professors or counselors who might have insight as to the benefits of journaling specifically or other coping techniques they use with their patients. We sent questions out to a few different individuals, but only ended up getting one respondent.

Jaclyn Kearns

We interviewed Jaclyn Kearns, a doctoral student at the U of R whose research focuses primarily on "executive function and emotion regulation deficits that may indicate individuals' move from suicidal thoughts to suicidal behavior". As such, she works mainly with high-risk patients, and mentioned to us:

"I don’t think I am the best person to answer these questions for two reasons-
  1. the majority of my patients are high-risk for suicide and/or with severe psychopathology so I practice within very structured treatment protocols;
  2. I don’t know the empirical evidence behind journaling and I have never suggested it to my patients."

This does highlight the fact that our product is not meant to replace traditional therapy methods and will likely be the most relevant to individuals who are not high-risk. We were nevertheless appreciative of Jackie's responses and the insight they gave as to how she works with patients. Our questions and her responses are detailed below.

Do you suggest journaling to patients/students? If so, what causes you to suggest it (specified disorders or certain criteria/personality)?

"I don’t think I have ever I suggest journaling to patients that I treat. I practice within structured therapies such as the Unified Protocol for the Transdiagnostic Treatment of Emotion Disorders, Dialectal Behavior Therapy, and Prolonged Exposure Therapy for PTSD. All of these therapies fall under the umbrella of Cognitive Behavioral Therapy.

"I may not be the best fit for providing information or insight on journaling as the majority of the patients I see are high-risk for suicide and/or with severe psychopathology; journaling isn’t something I would suggest for the type of patients that I see. I give my patients homework to practice skills that we have learned in-session (e.g., breathing exercises for PTSD treatment) as that will enhance what we cover in session.

"To get a better response, I might seek out someone who sees lower risk patients. Maybe reach out to someone at the University Counseling Center (UCC)? I would reach out the Brigid Cahill, PhD and ask her to suggest a counselor at UCC that you could speak to about this. I think someone at UCC may be more well-versed in this intervention than I am."

Do you see the benefits of journaling in your patients? If so, what are they?

"Journaling may be helpful for patients to understand the processes (e.g., thoughts, behaviors) and environmental factors that may underlie or contribute to their current symptoms or distress. However, this is merely a hypothesis - I don’t know the empirical evidence surrounding journaling as an intervention or adjunct intervention for patients with diagnosed psychiatric disorders. This is information that I would seek out in empirical literature. A librarian at UR could help you search empirical journals for evidence on possible benefits."

What other techniques do you suggest to your patients to try at home?

"Depending on what their diagnosis is, I often suggest the following: mindfulness- based exercises (e.g., meditation – can be completed with a phone app or a YouTube video), breathing exercises, dysfunctional thought logs (to help patients recognize “thinking traps”, to generate evidence for/against the thought, and to come up with alternative explanations), emotion induction exercises (to help patients practice being fully aware of your emotional experiences as they are occurring right now in the present moment, without labeling or judging), and various skills from dialectical behavior therapy (typically for patients that are at high-risk for suicide or patients who engage in nonsuicidal self-injury)."

Can you think of anything we have not included that would be beneficial to our users?
