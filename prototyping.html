Prototyping

Phase One


We started with a basic, low fidelity wireframe.

We depicted a few of the things we knew we'd need, like a login and account page.

We also sketched out:
  • The homepage, including the links we thought we'd need: creating a new journal, viewing old journals, a summary statistics page, a resources link, and a feedback page
  • A page to create a new journal entry
  • A page to view a list of past entries, each of which would lead to feedbac for the given entry
  • A stats page which would include graphs of certain metrics (like exercise) ovre time

Phase Two


Next, we created an interactive wireframe on InVision.

We decided on the color scheme and basic layout of the site.

We also decided how the journal creation page would be laid out, with certain features such as:
  • Sliders for measures of happiness, angriness, stress, and sleep
  • A few buttons asking about specific activities like exercise
  • A text area for journal writing

Next was the overall report page, where we planned to have graphs as well as some commentary on the user's aggregated data.

We ended up displaying graphs for three self reported mood indicators and sleep. For the feedback section we decided to look for correlations between reported moods and activities, and include a word cloud of the most common words used in a user's entries.

Over all, this prototype ended up being similar to the final version of the website in many ways, though the website itself was rated as easier to use in our user testing- for full results visit the evaluation page.
